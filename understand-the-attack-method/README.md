**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to research the technical details, objectives, and associated risks of the chosen attack method.

## Researching the technical details

Now that you've chosen your attack method, it's time to dig into the details. In this exercise, your goal is to develop a thorough technical understanding of how your chosen method works.

Start by gathering information on the specifics of your attack method. Some resources to consider:

- Online resources like OWASP, NIST, and SANS.
- Technical blogs and whitepapers.
- Research papers and conference presentations.

As you research, try to answer the following questions:

- What are the step-by-step mechanics of this attack? How does it actually work on a technical level?

- What tools or technologies are typically used to carry out this type of attack?

- What are the common vulnerabilities or weaknesses that this attack exploits?

- Are there any particular systems, applications, or data types that are especially vulnerable to this type of attack?

## Identifying objectives and risks

In addition to understanding the technical details, it's important to consider the broader context and implications of your chosen attack method.

- What are the typical objectives of attackers who use this method? Are they seeking to steal data, disrupt systems, make money, or something else?

- What are the potential impacts of a successful attack? Consider the risks to data confidentiality, integrity, and availability, as well as any business, reputational, or regulatory risks.

- How difficult is this attack to detect and defend against? What are some of the common challenges in preventing or mitigating this type of attack?

## Documenting your findings

As you research, be sure to document your findings. You might create a mind map, a set of notes, or even a preliminary outline for your final report. Any method works, as long as you're organizing the information in a way that helps you build a clear, comprehensive understanding of your attack method. You won't submit this as part of your lab deliverables.

By the end of this exercise, you should have a solid grasp of how your chosen attack method works, what it aims to achieve, and what risks it poses. You'll build on this foundation as you investigate a real-world incident involving this method.
