**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will present the key findings from the research and investigation in an engaging, informative presentation to peers.

## Preparing your presentation

Congratulations on completing your research report! The final step in this lab is to share your findings with your peers in a five-minute presentation.

Start by deciding on the key points you want to convey in your presentation. You won't be able to cover everything in your report, so focus on the most important and interesting findings.

A good presentation might include:

- A brief overview of the attack method and why it's significant.
- The key events and impacts of the real-world incident you investigated.
- The most important lessons learned and best practices for defense.
- Any particularly surprising or noteworthy findings from your research.

> 🧠 Because you won't be able to include all the details from your report in your presentation, ensure you include a link to your report on an appendix slide at the end of your presentation.

Create slides to support your presentation visually. Use images, diagrams, and bullet points to make your slides engaging and easy to follow. Your presentation should not be a script; instead, use the slides to highlight key points and support your verbal presentation.

## Delivering your presentation

When delivering your presentation, remember to:

- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace.
- Make eye contact with your audience.
- Use gestures and body language to engage your listeners.
- Allow time for questions and discussion.

## Engaging in discussion

After your presentation, engage with your peers in a discussion. Some prompts to consider:

- What similar experiences or insights can others share related to this attack method?
- What alternative perspectives or interpretations might there be of the incident you investigated?
- What further questions or areas for research does your presentation raise?

Remember, the goal isn't just to share your findings but to engage in a collaborative learning process with your peers.

## Reflecting on your learning

After the presentations and discussion, take some time to reflect on what you've learned throughout this lab. Consider:

- What new insights or skills have you gained through the research and investigation process?
- How has your understanding of the attack method and its real-world implications changed?
- What further questions or areas for learning are you curious to explore?

Through this process, you've developed valuable skills in research, investigation, reporting, and presentation skills.
